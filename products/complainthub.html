What runs today vs. the roadmap

Running today

Verified, in production — demo any of it live.
  • ✦Prompt reporting, any channel — staff or a site log a fault from web, email or WhatsApp; it lands in one unified queue in seconds.
  • ✦Rule-based routing — deterministic routing by asset, category and location to the right owner or technician.
  • ✦SLA auto-escalation — configurable time-based triggers escalate and auto-close so nothing quietly ages out.
  • ✦Accountability + 100% audit — role-guarded access, HTTP-only sessions, Zod-validated input and a full system of record: every touch on a ticket, on the record.
  • ✦Dashboards + $0 deploy — resolution-time and backlog analytics, one-command deploy to Always-Free cloud, self-updating replica.

On the roadmap

The autonomous AI crew — not shipped, never demoed as live.
  • ◆AI triage & monitoring — classify asset, fault type and severity; watch the queue 24/7 and alert before an SLA breaks. Falls back to today's rules on low confidence.
  • ◆Negligence radar — flag handlers whose tickets stall or breach SLA, so supervisors see the pattern instead of the excuse.
  • ◆Resolution copilot — drafts fix steps and repair-vs-replace calls from similar resolved cases, with the SLA clock surfaced.
  • ◆Parts & ops analyst — links faults to inventory, warns on low spares before a job, and briefs weekly downtime & cost trends. Plus multi-tenant + billing.

Honesty note: the AI crew is the vision layer. Sentiment/triage scaffolding exists but isn't wired — treat monitoring, negligence radar, copilot and predictive analytics as roadmap.

The role it replaces

A 24/7 facilities complaint desk.

Logging faults, chasing owners, escalating before SLAs break and keeping every asset accountable — the always-on grind of a maintenance-coordination team, run around the clock with a human in command before any dispatch.

1–2FTEs of coordination · ~16–24 hrs/day
The business case

Faulty equipment doesn't wait for a free agent. Every hour a fault sits unrouted is an hour of lost output, idle staff and a repair bill that grows. ComplaintHub makes the queue impossible to ignore — timely resolution, visible accountability and spares ready before the technician arrives — so a small problem stays a small problem, and operations keep running.
